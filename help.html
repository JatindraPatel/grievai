Step-by-step instructions to use the GrievAI Grievance Portal effectively.

🚀 Getting Started

GrievAI is designed to be simple and accessible for every citizen of India. You do not need to create an account or log in to file or track a complaint. Follow the guide below to use the portal effectively.

💡 Tip: Before filing a complaint, make sure your issue falls under a government department. Private sector complaints should be directed to the appropriate consumer forum.

📝 Step 1 – Filing a Complaint

You can file a complaint without logging in. Follow these steps:

  1. Click "Lodge Complaint" on the Home page or navigate to index.html#lodge.
  2. Fill in your Full Name and Mobile Number (mandatory).
  3. Optionally provide your Email Address for email notifications.
  4. Select the appropriate Department from the dropdown. If unsure, choose the closest match.
  5. Select your State or Union Territory.
  6. Enter a clear and concise Complaint Subject (e.g., "Water supply interrupted for 5 days in Sector 12").
  7. In the Complaint Description, provide all relevant details:
    • What happened and when
    • Location of the incident
    • Previous actions taken (if any)
    • Any evidence or reference numbers
  8. Check the declaration checkbox and click "Submit Complaint".
  9. A unique Complaint ID (format: GRIEVA/YYYY/XXXXXX) will be displayed. Save this ID — you will need it to track your complaint.
✅ An SMS with your Complaint ID is sent to your registered mobile number immediately after successful submission.

🔍 Step 2 – Tracking Your Complaint

Use your Complaint ID to track the status of your grievance at any time, without logging in:

  1. Go to the Track Complaint page.
  2. Enter your Complaint ID in the format GRIEVA/YYYY/XXXXXX.
  3. Optionally enter your registered mobile number for verification.
  4. Enter the captcha code shown on screen.
  5. Click "Track Complaint" to view the real-time status.

The tracking result shows:

  • Current status of the complaint
  • Assigned department and officer name
  • Complete activity timeline from registration to resolution
  • Expected resolution date

📊 Step 3 – Viewing Detailed Status

For a comprehensive status report including all officer remarks and actions taken:

  1. Go to the Status page.
  2. Enter your Complaint ID and click "Get Status".
  3. View the full report including timeline, officer details, and expected resolution date.
  4. Use the "Print Report" button to save or print a copy for your records.

🔐 Login Guide (Optional)

While login is not required to file or track complaints, registering gives you additional benefits:

  • View all your past complaints in one place (Citizen Dashboard)
  • Receive proactive notifications without entering ID each time
  • Faster re-filing with pre-filled personal details
  • Provide feedback on resolved complaints

To login, go to Login Page and select Citizen tab. Enter your mobile/email and OTP/password.

⚠️ Officer and Admin logins are restricted to authorized government personnel only. Unauthorized access attempts are logged and punishable under IT Act 2000.

💡 Tips for Faster Resolution

  • Be specific — mention exact dates, locations, and names in your complaint description.
  • Select correct department — wrong department routing causes delays. Use the Departments page for guidance.
  • Attach evidence — photos and documents significantly speed up the resolution process.
  • Save your Complaint ID — you will need it to track and follow up.
  • Provide accurate contact details — incorrect mobile numbers mean you won't receive SMS updates.
  • One complaint per issue — filing duplicate complaints may cause confusion and delays.
  • Follow up if no action — if no update in 15 days, contact the helpline for escalation.

⬆️ Escalation Process

If your complaint is not resolved within the stipulated time, you can escalate it:

  1. Auto-escalation: Complaints unresolved after 30 days are automatically escalated to senior officials.
  2. Manual escalation: Call helpline 1800-11-7781 with your Complaint ID to request escalation.
  3. Email escalation: Write to grievances@gov.in mentioning your Complaint ID and reason for escalation.
  4. Second appeal: If still unresolved, file a second appeal addressed to the Ministry's Grievance Officer.
