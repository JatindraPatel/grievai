❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about using the GrievAI Grievance Portal.

General

General Questions

GrievAI (Grievance Artificial Intelligence) is an AI-powered citizen grievance redressal portal developed and managed by the Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances & Pensions, Government of India. It is an extension of the CPGRAMS (Centralized Public Grievance Redress and Monitoring System) framework, enhanced with modern AI and NLP capabilities for faster complaint classification and resolution.

Any citizen of India can file a complaint on GrievAI. The portal covers grievances related to:

  • Central Government departments and ministries
  • State Government departments (all 29 states)
  • Union Territory administrations (all 8 UTs)
  • Public sector undertakings and autonomous bodies

There is no minimum age requirement, and complaints can be filed in multiple languages.

No, login is NOT required to file or track a complaint. The GrievAI portal allows citizens to:

  • File a complaint without registration or login
  • Track complaint status using the Complaint ID
  • View status reports without any account

Login is only required for Officers (to manage assigned complaints) and Administrators (for system management).

Yes, the GrievAI mobile application is available on both Google Play Store (Android) and Apple App Store (iOS). The app provides all the features of the web portal in a mobile-friendly interface including complaint filing, tracking, and push notifications for updates.

Filing Complaints

Filing a Complaint

Filing a complaint is simple and takes under 2 minutes:

  • Go to the Home page and click "Lodge Complaint"
  • Fill in your Name, Mobile Number, and Email (optional)
  • Select the relevant Department and State/UT
  • Enter a clear Subject and detailed Description
  • Accept the declaration and click "Submit Complaint"
  • Note down the Complaint ID provided — save it for tracking

The following information is mandatory:

  • Full Name
  • Mobile Number (for SMS notifications)
  • Department/Ministry concerned
  • State or Union Territory
  • Complaint Subject (brief title)
  • Complaint Description (minimum 50 characters)

Optional: Email address (for email updates), supporting documents (photos/PDFs).

Yes, you can attach supporting documents to strengthen your complaint. The portal accepts:

  • Images: JPG, PNG (max 2MB each)
  • Documents: PDF (max 5MB)
  • Maximum 3 attachments per complaint

Supporting evidence significantly increases the chance of faster and accurate resolution.

The following types of complaints are generally not accepted on this portal:

  • Complaints pending in courts (subjudice matters)
  • Suggestions or policy matters
  • RTI-related requests (use rtionline.gov.in)
  • Complaints against private companies or individuals
  • Matters that are purely political in nature
  • Anonymous complaints without any verifiable contact information
Tracking

Tracking & Status

You can track your complaint in two ways:

  • Online: Visit the Track Complaint page, enter your Complaint ID and security captcha.
  • SMS: Send TRACK <ComplaintID> to 56677
  • Helpline: Call 1800-11-7781 and provide your Complaint ID

No login is required for any of these methods.

Resolution timelines as per Government of India policy:

  • Normal complaints: Up to 30 working days
  • Urgent/Priority: 7 to 15 working days
  • Emergency (Health, Safety): Within 24–48 hours
  • Police complaints: Maximum 7 working days

If no action is taken within 30 days, the complaint is automatically escalated to senior officials.

A "Resolved" status means the assigned officer has taken action and submitted a closure report. You will receive an SMS/email with the resolution details.

If you are not satisfied with the resolution, you can:

  • Request a reopen within 30 days of closure
  • File an appeal to the senior officer
  • Contact the helpline: 1800-11-7781
Technical

Technical & Account Questions

If you have lost your Complaint ID, you can retrieve it by:

  • Checking the SMS sent to your registered mobile number at the time of filing
  • Checking your Email (if provided during filing)
  • Calling the helpline 1800-11-7781 with your name and mobile number
  • Logging into your Citizen account (if you were registered) at the dashboard

GrievAI is compatible with all modern browsers:

  • Google Chrome (recommended) – version 90+
  • Mozilla Firefox – version 85+
  • Microsoft Edge – version 90+
  • Apple Safari – version 14+
  • Opera – version 76+

Internet Explorer is not supported. Please use a modern browser for the best experience.

Yes, your data is completely secure. The portal uses:

  • SSL/TLS 256-bit encryption for all data transmission
  • Data stored on NIC (National Informatics Centre) secure government servers
  • Compliant with IT Act 2000 and National Data Protection guidelines
  • No data is shared with third parties for commercial purposes
  • Regular VAPT (Vulnerability Assessment & Penetration Testing) conducted

For full details, read our Privacy & Security Policy.
